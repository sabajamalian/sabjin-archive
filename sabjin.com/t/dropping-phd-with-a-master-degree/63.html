Dropping PhD with a Master Degree

nasringoldasht (Nasrin Goldasht) July 14, 2022, 7:25pm 1

I am a PhD student and have two more years before graduation. There is a great full-time job opportunity for me at the moment but will require me to drop my PhD program and leave my international student Visa status.

Is this possible or do I have to wait until I finish the PhD program? How can I legally drop my PhD program and receive a Master’s degree? I want to keep my legal status in the United States as I start my full-time career.

mohammad (Mohammad Virayesh) July 15, 2022, 5:59pm 2

It is theoretically possible. In reality, it depends on the school and your Ph.D. advisor. The advisor could make it extremely difficult if not onboard. Make sure to read the terms of your Ph.D. program before you begin.
Also, remember that dropping a Ph.D. program could make it difficult for you to come back to Academia later unless you have a good explanation.

farshid (Farshid Tayebi) July 19, 2022, 6:38pm 3

Dropping a PhD program is absolutely legal. If you have passed enough credits, you should be able to get a Master’s degree without any problems. Consult with the international center at your university to learn about the legal issues and the possibility of issuing an OPT for you.
